STEVE FRANKO

Rising Fall Partner & Master Colorist

Specializing in color grading television commercials, web ads and corporate promos, Steve has collaborated with top advertising agencies and individual clients alike. His client list includes American Airlines, AT&T and McDonald’s, plus agencies such as The Richards Group, JWT and Ackerman McQueen. He has colored hundreds of commercials, including a number of award‐winning ads recognized both nationally and internationally. Just this year, Tabasco’s “Mosquito” ad was recently named by USA Today as one of the Top 20 Super Bowl ads of all time.


In a way, Steve’s career at Rising Fall actually began over 35 years ago, when in 1981, he began freelancing with a small company called Video Post. New ownership led to a new company name later that same year, and Video Post became Video Post and Transfer (VPT). When VPT established Dallas as home to the first film Telecine in the entire Southwest region, Steve and VPT owner Neil Feldman together introduced film and video color correction to the area’s post‐production industry. In 2006 Steve helped launch VPT’s new satellite company, And Transfer, where again he mastered cutting‐edge technology utilizing the first Digital Intermediate color correction system in the Southwest. In 2011 Steve joined new ownership to become a partner in And Transfer — now Rising Fall.


Prior to joining Video Post, Steve worked in all facets of production, post production and live television at Mobile Color Incorporated (MCI), the Southwest’s largest production/post production facility of its time. It was here that Steve first chose to focus on post production and began to hone his skills as an editor and colorist.


A native Texan, Steve was born and raised in Fort Worth. He earned his degree in Radio/TV from North Texas State University, now the University of North Texas, in Denton.
